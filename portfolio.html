One of dozens of campaigns I created for WBUR’s fundraising. During my time there, we increased the amount raised through the mail by more than TEN times. This campaign illustrated our central theme -- by contributing through the mail, you could drastically reduce on-air fundraising, a benefit that every listener would support.

Food for Thought campaign sample letter

Envelope

Food for thought: Your $75 pledge to WBUR is less than $.20 per day ... less than a daily newspaper

Letter cover

More food for thought: With your help right now, you’ll hear more news on WBUR all year round.

Letter copy

Dear WBUR Supporter,

As a supporter of WBUR, you may have noticed something new during the last year—less on-air fundraising.

During the past year, we reduced our on-air fundraising significantly…including cutting our most recent fundraiser from 10 days down to 4!

In the coming year, we will do even more. For example, with your help, we intend to eliminate next summer’s fundraiser entirely. And we hope to reduce the length of our 2 remaining on-air fundraisers…starting by shortening the upcoming fall fundraiser by 25%.

We can do so only with your help. To reduce the fundraisers, we need to increase the amount of money we raise through the mail. Which is why I hope we can count on your to make an additional pledge now.

Keep in mind that WBUR really is a bargain, compared to any other news source you can name. Newspapers? If you get a paper every day, you probably spend about $200 per year. Cable TV? Generally about $40 per month. And, while other radio stations don’t charge you directly, you still pay for their programming in the form of commercials—over 180 hours per year, if you listen 2 hours per day.

Only WBUR brings you commercial-free, in-depth news 17 hours every weekday. Only WBUR brings you in-depth news anywhere you are—your car, your kitchen, your portable stereo, anywhere. And only WBUR lets you decide how much to pay for the service you depend on—$75, $100, $150, whatever you think it’s worth.

Don’t forget: for your pledge of $150, you’ll receive 2 tickets to “Food for Thought,” our NPR Brunch at 12 Noon on December 4th at the Sheraton Boston.

Featuring Scott Simon, Robert Siegel and Mara Liasson, “Food for Thought” is a unique opportunity to hear inside information and entertaining tidbits from 3 of NPR’s most popular personalities. Tickets are $75 each.

You’ll have a wonderful time— and support WBUR in the process. Let me remind you, however—this event always sells out, so order your tickets now to avoid missing out.

This is your last chance to pledge and reduce the length of the on-air fundraiser! I hope we can count on you to help us bring you more news— and less fundraising—all year round.

Sincerely,

Jane Christo,

General Manager, WBUR

P.S. Your contribution goes even farther when you participate in a corporate matching program. Please see your company’s human resources department for details.

Wellesley College

Wellesley College’s fundraising efforts include a series of letters, customized by audience, along with other elements including brochures, lift notes, etc. These are supported by ads in Wellesley’s alumnae magazine, email blasts, follow-ups, and thank you notes. In a very poor year for fundraising, these efforts actually increased donations.


Copy

Dear [First Name],

As the incoming Chair of Annual Giving, I’d like to take a moment to explain why I support Wellesley as both a donor and a volunteer.

Wellesley opened the entire world for me. I arrived on campus as a naive young woman from Oklahoma. At Wellesley, my professors and classmates challenged me to believe that I could do anything I wanted. Even today, when I hit a roadblock, I re-visit that “Wellesley place” in my mind and find inspiration, strength, and a “get-it-done” attitude.

As an alumna, I support Wellesley today so that other young women may have a similar experience, to be empowered to pursue their dreams and to make a difference. For example, consider what these current students have accomplished:

  • *Linnea Herzog ’12 organized a book collection for libraries in Turkmenistan and Philadelphia inner city schools.
  • *Phoebe Hanlder ’12 actively participated in and co-authored an NIH-funded cancer study.
  • *Roshan Alemi ’12 interned at the Center for Sustainable Development in Iran, an NGO that assists nomads.

The success of young women like these benefits us all. They are making a difference, which both reinforces the strength of the Wellesley network and reaffirms the value of our Wellesley education. Our support of Wellesley through Annual Giving paves the way.

Today, I am asking you to renew your support of Annual Giving, which has a direct impact on students:

  • *Annual Giving allows Wellesley to offer generous financial aid, maintain a “need blind” admission policy, and in turn attract the most deserving students.
  • *Ongoing financial strength allows Wellesley to nurture its intellectual community, the cornerstone of academic excellence.

One last point: alumnae support made it possible for us to attend Wellesley, whether or not we received financial aid. That’s because the true cost of a Wellesley education is much higher than what students pay in tuition (currently, about 62% higher). The gap is closed by sources like Annual Giving. So by supporting Annual Giving, we are “repaying” those alumnae who were there for us. It is a Wellesley tradition that has gone on for many generations.

Thank you for being generous last year. Please take this opportunity to once again help Wellesley educate the next generation of women who will make a difference in the world.

(signature)

Sandra Yeager ’86

Chair, Annual Giving

P.S. Wellesley students are accomplishing extraordinary things every day. Let’s change the lives of today’s students!
